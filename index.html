History

Pre-History

Acoustic Guitars

The earliest guitars were created by cavemen by accident when using twine to carry a drum. When the twine was plucked, the cavegirls went wild and the first Groupies were invented.

In fact, several innovations were cemented in this early era, including:

  1. Dodgy Managers
  2. Dubious "Contracts"
  3. Guitarist Ego

Post-History

Electric Guitars

Once the guitar was refined over the centuries, Thomas Edison could claim credit to electrify the instrument, unfortunately killing several of his lab assistants along the way. It was only when Ronald Pickup came up with a coil of copper wire to translate the string vibation into an electric waveform that the instrument was safe to play.

And an amp...

Types

Single Coil Pickup

This, as the name suggests, is a length of wire wrapped around a single pole, used on Fender guitars.

Humbucker Pickup

A dual pole pickup provides a warmer tone, used on such guitars as a Les Paul or Rickenbacker.
